Discussion

The bar plot shows how the total number of visitors varies across locations. We can see that some sites receive higher levels of activity than others, indicating that human use is not distributed evenly across the Delta. The stacked bars are helful because they show that different visitor types contribute differently at each location. For example, some sites seem to have more boat activity, while others have more cars or bank anglers, suggesting differences in how each site is accessed and used, which may be better explained looking at are map.

The map provides important context for the patterns we see in the bar plot by showing where each restoration location is in the Delta. The sites are spread across a wide geographic area, which helps see reason of visitation type. for example locations that are more accessible or closer to people may receive more visitors, while more remote sites may see less activity. Which is where i’d be going.

Together, the plot and the map complement each other by combining quantitative patterns with location context. The plot summarizes how visitation differs across sites and visitor types and the map helps explain these differences by showing where the sites are located.
